Time series

Time series are used for communicating data that changes over time, where the x axis [typically] of the chart is specially designed for the time scale appropriate for your data:

  • Inter-day datasets (e.g. stock prices, thermostat measurements) require hour:minute:second formatted axes.
  • Annualised datasets (e.g. company performance numbers, global temperature) require month:day formatted axes
  • Historical datasets require axes that span both BC and AD dates.
  • Timelines for historical/geological periods require axes that allow some overlap and are a specialised type of time series visualisation.

Before starting to design your time series visualisations ensure that your data is well formatted for visualisation, most tools expect specific formats for dates/times. The most general purpose standard for formatting dates/times is ISO 8601, which can be summarised into the following guidance:

  • Date format: yyyy-mm-dd
  • Time format: hh:mm:ss
  • Combined date and time (UTC time zone) format: yyyy-mm-ddThh:mm:ss+00:00
